Filet Mignon with Rich Balsamic Glaze

Today, we'll be making a widely popular dish all across the world which is steak. But to be more specific, this steak originated from the French with its popular name the "Filet Mignon"!

This recipe is intended to create a serving size of 2 and will roughly take 15 minutes, where 5 of those minutes will be in preparing the dish, and another 10 will be in cooking it.

Ingredients

  • Two Steaks
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Balsamic Vinegar
  • Dry Red Wine

Steps

  1. Season the Steaks: Sprinkle the filet mignon with salt and pepper
  2. Sear the Steaks: Sear the steaks in a very hot pan until they're browned on both sides
  3. Make the Sauce: Reduce the heat to medium-low, then add the vinegar and wine. Cover and cook for about four minutes. Flip the steaks, baste with the sauce, and continue cooking for about four more minutes.
  4. Serve the Steaks: Place the steaks on two warmed plates. Spoon the sauce over the meat and serve immediately.
